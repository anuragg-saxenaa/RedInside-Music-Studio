The Key Insight: Everything Belongs to a Track

In the current design, you pick a step (Artwork, Mastering, etc.) and then wonder "which song am I doing this for?" In world-class tools, you pick a track first, then all tools for that track appear together. Artwork, edit, mastering — all live as actions on the track, not as separate menu items.

Where Does Artwork Live? (The Answer)

NOW (confusing)

Artwork is Step 3. User generates music, then has to manually go to a separate "Artwork" step. No visual connection between the song and its cover.

PHASE 4 (clear)

Artwork thumbnail lives in the right panel, always visible for the selected track. One click → opens the CREATE tab. Feels like album art belongs to the track, not a separate feature.

Suno / BandLab / Splice — What They All Share

Every top tool is track-first, not feature-first. You see your track library on the left. You select a track. The right side shows everything about that track: waveform, artwork, metadata, actions. The studio tools (edit, master, export) are verbs applied to the selected track — not separate destinations you navigate to.

That's what Phase 4 does. The 5 tabs (WRITE / SOUNDS / CREATE / CRAFT / RELEASE) stay in context of the selected project. The right panel keeps the selected track + its artwork always visible. Playlist in the left sidebar organises your library.
